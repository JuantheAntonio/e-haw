About Us

Project E-HAW (E-System for Harmonizing Actions and Welfare) is developed to bridge this gap by providing a digital platform that prioritizes efficiency, confidentiality, and accountability. Project E-HAW is a web-based reporting system specifically designed for the residents and officials of Barangay Kamputhaw, Cebu City. This platform serves as a digital bridge that allows citizens to report community issues that range from environmental hazards, like illegal dumping, to public safety concerns, such as drug-related activities.

Current reporting methods in the barangay often rely on physical presence at the hall or informal messages that eventually get lost in the shuffle. This presents problems, such as residents will find the reporting process tedious, since it often requires lengthy explanations or face-to-face interviews, and the fear of retaliation prevents them from reporting crimes or social issues. Without a guaranteed confidential channel, critical problems go unaddressed.

Project E-HAW revolutionizes the 'sumbong' culture by focusing on a 'capture and send' methodology. By integrating GPS auto-tagging and categorized photo uploads, the system reduces the reporting process to just a few taps. This ensures that the Barangay Hall receives high-quality, actionable information (photos and exact locations) without the resident needing to write long essays.

Beyond its technological features, Project E-HAW is built for the local context. The name reflects a commitment to address the systemic problems of the community through modern intervention. It is a tool for transparency, accountability, and a cleaner, safer Kamputhaw.
